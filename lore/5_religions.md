title: Beral, the Creator
subTitle: The distant Mother of the Gods, first shaper of Amra and source of the pantheon's authority.
body:
## Overview

Beral is revered as the mother of the gods, the First, and the creator whose power shaped Amra and gave authority to the pantheon. She is not a household patron in the way other deities are. Most people encounter her through creation myths, origin prayers, and invocations spoken before addressing one of her divine children.

## Divine Role

Beral's place in Amra's mythology is distant, vast, and difficult for mortal thought to contain. She creates, orders, and preserves cosmic balance, but rarely intervenes in mortal affairs or even in the disputes of the gods unless the stability of creation itself is threatened.

She is often imagined as a luminous being capable of creating or destroying worlds. Mystics warn that this is only a mercy-image: a form small enough for mortal minds to survive.

## Worship and Mystery

Most temples honor Beral indirectly, naming her as the source behind divine power. Secretive religious orders and monastic seekers claim to commune with her through prayer, meditation, and ecstatic silence. Whether those claims are revelation, metaphor, or dangerous pride remains a matter of faith.

## Campaign Use

- Use Beral when the story touches creation, cosmic balance, or mysteries older than the gods.
- Let her silence matter: divine absence can be as frightening as divine command.
- Make direct contact rare, ambiguous, and costly enough that witnesses disagree about what happened.

title: Adate
subTitle: Goddess of lies, illusions, intrigue, and necessary deceit, feared as both poison and balance.
body:
## Overview

Adate is the goddess of lies, illusions, intrigue, and deceit. Many condemn her as malign, yet theologians argue that Beral created her as a counterweight against dangerous extremes of truth, certainty, and righteousness.

## Divine Role

Adate embodies manipulation, disguise, seduction, secrecy, and the power of falsehood to alter reality. She is feared because her influence reaches courts, temples, markets, lovers, spies, and rebels. She is respected because some truths destroy what a careful lie can preserve.

Her cult attracts the ambitious, the hunted, the politically trapped, and those who believe conventional virtue has failed them.

## Cult and Rites

Adate's followers work in secrecy. Their rituals are hidden from public judgment and often involve illusion, mind-clouding rites, extracted secrets, offerings, and sacrifices made to purchase influence or concealment.

The faithful believe lies are tools, not accidents. A lie may be a weapon, veil, mercy, trap, or prayer, depending on who speaks it and what price they pay.

## Campaign Use

- Use Adate to complicate simple truth-versus-lie morality.
- Let her worshipers solve real problems by unacceptable means.
- Pair Adate with Honos when oaths, trials, testimony, or sacred law become dangerous.

title: Basaj
subTitle: God of nature, beasts, and the untamed wilderness, guardian of harmony and primal consequence.
body:
## Overview

Basaj is the god of nature, beasts, and the untamed wilderness. Druids, hunters, wanderers, beast-keepers, and communities living close to the wild honor him as guardian, teacher, and judge.

## Divine Role

Beral created Basaj to help mortals understand their place within nature rather than above it. He heals, commands weather, guides beasts, blesses harvests, and punishes those who defile the living world.

Basaj is benevolent, but not tame. Legends give him the form of wolf, bear, and other primal shapes when the natural world is threatened.

## Clergy and Rites

Basaj's clergy perform rites in forests, mountains, rivers, and other living places. Acolytes may undergo a solitary night in the wild to prove courage and kinship with nature. Fertility rituals celebrate harvest, animal life, and seasonal renewal through song, dance, fruit, flowers, and beast-like movement.

Healing rites use plants and herbs while calling on Basaj to let nature share its restorative power.

## Campaign Use

- Use Basaj when civilization takes more from the land than it can repay.
- Make his blessings practical: food, weather, healing, animal warnings, or safe passage.
- Let his wrath feel ecological before it feels theological.

title: Brom
subTitle: God of forging, metalworking, engineering, craft, and the order made possible by skilled hands.
body:
## Overview

Brom is the god of forging, metalworking, engineering, and creative skill. Smiths, builders, artificers, masons, armorers, and inventors honor him whenever raw material becomes useful beauty.

## Divine Role

Beral made Brom to teach mortals metallurgy and engineering. His faith treats craft as sacred discipline: a well-made hinge, bridge, blade, plow, or engine can bring order and safety into a dangerous world.

Brom is passionate and exacting, but also playful. Tales credit him with impossible contraptions, clever machines, and jokes built into devices that still work centuries later.

## Clergy and Rites

Brom's rites take place in forges, workshops, foundries, and construction sites. Offerings include metal, tools, jewelry, and the first finished work from a major commission.

His clergy bless difficult projects, sing work songs, dance around anvils, and ask for skill rather than miracles. Theft of craft knowledge, shoddy work passed off as mastery, and sabotage of honest labor are grave offenses.

## Campaign Use

- Use Brom when a crafted object carries lineage, faith, or political importance.
- Let temples of Brom function as workshops, guildhalls, courts, and arsenals.
- Make a masterpiece require rare materials, moral compromise, or cooperation between rival craftspeople.

title: Erio
subTitle: God of death, oblivion, decay, and disease, feared as final judge and honored as guide of souls.
body:
## Overview

Erio reigns over death, oblivion, decay, and disease. Mortals fear him because every life enters his shadow, but his faith insists that death without guidance is worse than death itself.

## Divine Role

Beral created Erio to guide souls through the veil into the realm beyond. In the Realm of Death, he is said to judge the departed and determine whether they find rest, paradise, or oblivion.

His power is terrifying because it is inevitable. Yet within Amra's divine order, Erio is not merely cruelty. He is the keeper of endings, memory, mourning, and the boundary that gives life shape.

## Clergy and Rites

Erio's priests are few, solemn, and often socially isolated. They care for the dying, tend cemeteries and catacombs, prepare bodies, conduct funerals, and guide grieving families through ritual acceptance.

Their holiest rite is the Last Word, spoken at a deathbed as the soul loosens from the body. The priest offers comfort, prayer, and farewell so the dead do not cross alone.

## Campaign Use

- Use Erio when death must be handled with dignity rather than spectacle.
- Let his clergy know truths that living society avoids.
- Make burial, plague, undeath, or soul judgment a serious religious matter.

title: Ethoras
subTitle: God of light, life, healing, creation, and crops, whose corrupted Castell inquisition betrays his gifts.
body:
## Overview

Ethoras is god of light, life, healing, creation, and crops. He is praised in fields, hospitals, homes, and roadside shrines wherever people ask that wounds close, seeds rise, and darkness lose its claim.

## Divine Role

Ethoras is said to have shaped the first rays of sun and stars, giving the dead celestial guides and the living warmth, fertility, and growth. His worshipers believe divine light can heal sickness, reveal corruption, and nourish the world.

His true faith is life-giving, restorative, and grateful. Its rituals often occur in fields, gardens, places of natural beauty, and among the sick or injured.

## Castell's Broken Light

In Castell, Ethoras' cult has been corrupted into a fanatical inquisition. Zealots persecute those outside their narrow doctrine, turning a god of healing and crops into a symbol of oppression.

The source lore is clear that these inquisitors have lost Ethoras' favor. Their violence is not holy law but a perversion waiting to be confronted by faithful reformers, rebels, and victims.

## Campaign Use

- Use Ethoras to separate genuine light from authoritarian certainty.
- Make Castell's inquisition a religious crisis, not proof that Ethoras is cruel.
- Let healing miracles expose who has been harmed by institutions claiming virtue.

title: Honos
subTitle: God of duty, truth, honor, and vigilance, patron of guardians and sworn protectors.
body:
## Overview

Honos is god of duty, truth, honor, and vigilance. Sentinels, warriors, judges, oathkeepers, guards, and protectors of the vulnerable invoke him when resolve must outlast fear.

## Divine Role

Beral formed Honos from righteousness, bravery, and the need for reliable guardianship. His symbol is a steadfast shield marked by a crossing sword, a reminder that defense is sacred when it protects the innocent.

Honos demands sincerity and fairness. He despises treachery, false oaths, and the deliberate corruption of trust.

## Oaths and Guardians

Honos has fewer worshipers than some gods, but his influence reaches far. Many who never enter his temples still swear by his name when taking office, guarding a gate, signing a treaty, or promising protection.

In peace, his faithful are pillars of justice. In war, they become commanders, defenders, and last lines against chaos.

## Campaign Use

- Use Honos when a vow creates both strength and constraint.
- Pair him with Adate when truth, testimony, and deception collide.
- Let his faithful face hard choices where every honorable option costs something.

title: Maeve
subTitle: Goddess of music, art, beauty, passion, desire, peace, and harmony.
body:
## Overview

Maeve is goddess of music, the arts, beauty, passion, desire, peace, and harmony. Artists, musicians, poets, lovers, performers, diplomats, and makers of beautiful things honor her.

## Divine Role

Beral created Maeve to awaken creativity and passion in mortal souls. Her golden lyre symbolizes music and art, but her faith teaches that beauty is not decoration. Beauty can reconcile, inspire, seduce, reveal, and sometimes disarm violence before it begins.

Maeveites believe beauty exists in all things and that creating or appreciating art can become a path toward the divine.

## Church of Beauty

Maeve's temples and shrines are designed to inspire devotion through beauty. Her priests and priestesses, called Bearers of Beauty, manage sacred spaces and lead ceremonies of music, dance, poetry, painting, and celebration.

Her church is not based on lineage. Leaders are chosen for demonstrated artistic excellence and their ability to inspire love of beauty in others.

## Campaign Use

- Use Maeve when art carries political, romantic, or divine power.
- Let beauty become a force for peace without making it harmless.
- Make a performance, painting, or song the key to memory, diplomacy, or conflict.

title: Necis
subTitle: God of war, strife, conquest, domination, and the brutal ambition that hides beneath civilization.
body:
## Overview

Necis is god of war, strife, conquest, and domination. His followers believe power and glory are won through blood, discipline, intimidation, and the defeat of weaker rivals.

## Divine Role

Though born of the same divine source as the other gods, Necis is marked by hatred, ambition, and hunger for authority. He teaches mortals weaponcraft, conquest, and the idea that violence can impose order on the unwilling.

Many fear and despise him, but soldiers, tyrants, conquerors, and those desperate for victory still call on his name.

## Blood Rites and War Cults

Necis' temples often stand near fortresses, castles, arenas, and military grounds. His rites include martial training, blood offerings, oaths of fealty, human sacrifice, duels, and tournaments meant to display strength before the god of war.

His worshipers often clash with other faiths, and the resulting violence only strengthens his reputation.

## Campaign Use

- Use Necis when ambition turns faith into conquest.
- Let his cults offer real strength at a terrible moral cost.
- Make victory in his name create new enemies, debts, and atrocities.

title: Nithra
subTitle: Goddess of magic, knowledge, science, moons, and night, patron of seekers who study the hidden order.
body:
## Overview

Nithra is goddess of magic, knowledge, science, the moons, and the night. Wizards, sages, astronomers, teachers, archivists, and dangerous experimenters all seek her favor.

## Divine Role

Beral created Nithra to preside over hidden knowledge and the forces that let mortals understand or reshape reality. She governs lunar mystery, eclipses, star patterns, and the boundary between wisdom and obsession.

Her symbol is often a crescent moon embracing a shadowed orb. Many believe she reveals truth only to those willing to sit patiently in darkness.

## Clergy and Rites

Nithra's clergy are sages, wizards, scholars, and mystics who combine ritual, study, magical relics, and meditation beneath moonlight. They perform complex ceremonies to seek insight, summon counsel, or align themselves with lunar power.

The Festival of the Moons, held around the autumnal equinox, honors her influence over magic and the world through lunar phases.

## Campaign Use

- Use Nithra when knowledge is powerful, incomplete, or dangerous.
- Tie her rites to moon phases, eclipses, towers, schools, and forbidden archives.
- Let her clergy solve mysteries while creating new ethical risks.

title: Odine
subTitle: Goddess of sea, sky, wind, tides, storms, sailors, fishermen, and the balance of calm and fury.
body:
## Overview

Odine is goddess of the sea and sky, guardian of sailors, fishermen, tides, winds, rain, and storms. Her name is spoken in gratitude for calm waters and in fear when the horizon darkens.

## Divine Role

Beral created Odine to govern the balance between ocean depths and open sky. She commands wind and tide, protects those who depend on the sea, and embodies both serene grace and destructive tempest.

Her realm is often imagined as an enchanted underwater kingdom where sea creatures answer her will.

## Clergy and Rites

Odine's clerics wear amulets marked with waves. Fishing families and sailors call on her through seasonal rites, voyage blessings, and offerings cast into water.

A baptismal rite marks children born into seafaring families: a shell is thrown into the sea in Odine's name. The Festival of Storms, held on the winter solstice, asks her protection through the dangerous season.

## Campaign Use

- Use Odine whenever travel depends on weather, tide, and courage.
- Let her blessings guide ships, reveal sea omens, or calm impossible storms.
- Make her fury feel natural, divine, and politically disruptive all at once.
