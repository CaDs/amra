title: Arcane Magic
subTitle: The mortal study of arcane energy, inherited from dragons and refined by elves under Nithra’s influence.
body:
## Overview

It is whispered that in ancient times, dragons alone were capable of wielding this enigmatic energy, their innate mastery of the arcane ingrained within the core of their very being. Born with a profound connection to the magic that courses through Amra, dragons possess an unparalleled ability to shape and control the arcane, an ability that remains elusive to lesser species.

Elves, however, possess a natural affinity for the arcane that allows them to grasp the complexities of manipulating this magical energy with greater ease than most. Their connection to nature and the elemental spirits of the world instills within them an understanding and respect for the forces that govern their world, allowing them to better attune themselves to the magic that weaves through it.

Inquisitive by nature, the elves rapidly absorb knowledge and skills, their minds ever-thirsty for the mysteries of the arcane. It is said that when Nithra, the goddess of Magic, brought the elves to Amra, they swiftly discovered the means to channel the arcane through various schools of sorcery. Their innate affinity for magic, combined with their capacity for learning, allowed them to become formidable wizards and sorcerers, their mastery of the arcane unparalleled.

Elves also possess a unique ability to harmonize with other magical beings, such as fairies and elemental spirits. This harmony grants them a deeper understanding of magic, enabling them to work with it more effectively.

To maintain balance in the world and to guide mortals in their pursuit of arcane knowledge, BeraL created Nithra, the goddess of magic and the divine patroness of magicians and sorcerers. Her influence spans the entirety of Amra, and her worship is a common practice among those seeking to master the arcane arts.

Devotees of Nithra often establish temples and sanctuaries in her honor, dedicating themselves to the study and practice of magic in its myriad forms. It is believed that those who receive Nithra's blessings gain greater control over arcane energy, enabling them to cast more potent spells and exert greater command over magical effects.

In accordance with Nithra's designs, the elves of Amra forged various schools of magic to refine their command over the arcane and to share their wisdom with other races. Each school delves into a distinct aspect of magic, specializing in particular spells and incantations. Among these hallowed institutions are:

### The creation of the towers of High Magic

During the gilded age of the elves, a great conclave of esteemed wizards and sorcerers convened to address a growing concern. Magic, a force of immense potential, held the power to uplift or destroy, and the elves feared that, unchecked, the burgeoning use of arcane arts by other races could plunge their world into chaos.

It was in the hallowed halls of their secret council that the concept of the Towers of High Magic took form - majestic, towering structures that would serve as bastions of knowledge, where seekers of the arcane from all corners of the world could gather and learn the secrets of magic under the watchful guidance of experienced mentors. For years, the council's wizards toiled in the shadows, crafting intricate plans and architectural blueprints for each tower, until at last, the time came to bring their grand vision to life.

The Towers of High Magic were veritable marvels, colossal stone monuments with imposing walls and a vast central courtyard where acolytes could hone their magical prowess. Nestled within each tower lay extensive libraries and laboratories, sanctuaries where wizards could safely study and experiment with the arcane.

Strategically placed throughout the continent of Amra, the towers were erected in major cities and regions, as well as in remote, secluded locales. The council's wizards ensured that, from any point in Amra, no more than a few days' journey would be required to reach the nearest tower, offering convenient access to magical knowledge.

Each tower bore a distinctive design, reflecting the unique needs and abilities of the wizards and sorcerers residing in the region. Towers nestled in mountainous terrain boasted reinforced walls and heating systems to weather the biting cold, while those gracing coastal areas were constructed with water-resistant materials and grand windows to showcase the breathtaking ocean vistas.

To safeguard the sanctity of the towers and ensure only those adept in the arcane could enter, the council devised a stringent admissions process. Prospective students were required to be sponsored by a trained mage and pass a rigorous examination before being granted access to a tower.

Once accepted, students dedicated years to the study and practice of magic under the tutelage of seasoned wizards and sorcerers. The curriculum within each tower was designed to be intensive, cultivating responsible and skilled wielders of the arcane arts.

The Towers of High Magic emerged as epicenters of arcane wisdom, drawing the most gifted wizards and sorcerers from across Amra to exchange knowledge and collaborate on magical endeavors. An agreement was reached to distribute the towers based on the size and population of each region, with larger, more populated areas receiving a greater number of towers.

A wizard or sorcerer from each region was appointed as the custodian of their respective Tower of High Magic, overseeing its teachings and ensuring that only those who demonstrated aptitude and understanding were granted access to study within its hallowed halls.

A system of regulation and evaluation was implemented to maintain the integrity of the towers and their caretakers. Inspectors traversed the land, assessing the performance of the appointed wizards and sorcerers, ensuring they adhered to established standards and imparted their knowledge effectively.

Over time, the Towers of High Magic became the preeminent hubs of magical learning and discovery in all of Amra. The world's most formidable wielders of the arcane convened within these towers, exchanging groundbreaking theories and techniques while mentoring the next generation of mages.

Regrettably, even with the establishment of the towers, instances of misused magic and power abuse persisted. In such cases, the custodians of the towers were duty-bound to intervene, thwarting those who exploited magic for malevolent purposes.

To further deter misuse of the arcane, a code of conduct was established for all who studied within the Towers of High Magic. This code outlined the expected behaviors and ethical practices of wizards and sorcerers, instilling within them the importance of utilizing magic solely for benevolent purposes and never for nefarious ends.

The Towers of High Magic not only served as repositories of arcane knowledge but also as crossroads where mages from all corners of Amra could convene, exchange wisdom, and collaborate on ambitious magical undertakings. The presence of these esteemed institutions catalyzed a surge in the number of practitioners of the arcane arts, fostering a deeper understanding and wider application of magic across Amra.

Nevertheless, not all embraced the existence of these towers and the proliferation of magic they enabled. Some held the belief that magic should be the exclusive domain of those born with an innate gift, arguing that the construction of these towers granted access to the arcane to any who possessed the time and resources to study its secrets. Furthermore, concerns arose regarding the potential abuse of magic and the perceived inadequacy of regulatory measures.

Despite such apprehensions, the Towers of High Magic continue to stand as integral components of Amra's magical landscape. Their existence has facilitated the widespread dissemination of arcane knowledge, allowing aspiring mages to pursue their studies in a safe and structured environment. And while magic inherently harbors potential dangers, the presence of these towers has ensured that the power of the arcane is harnessed for the greater good throughout the world.

## Campaign Use

- Treat arcane study as a question of access, lineage, discipline, and risk.
- Let independent magicians challenge the authority of towers and formal schools.
- Use dragons and elves as living reminders that mortal magic began with older powers.

title: Divine Magic
subTitle: Power drawn from gods, nature spirits, faith, conviction, and the disciplined channeling of Ki.
body:
## Overview

In the mystical realm of Amra, the tapestry of magic is also woven with threads of divine origin. Divine magic, as it is known, centers on forging a bond with the gods, invoking their celestial might to seek guidance and protection, and harnessing their power to cast spells capable of purifying, blessing, or cursing others.

Divine magic in Amra is born from various origins, each rich in its own history and tradition. The first and most widely accepted source of divine magic is believed to flow directly from the gods themselves. These celestial beings govern the cosmos, maintaining a delicate balance, and they are known to bestow their divine power upon those who dutifully serve and venerate them. Priests and paladins who dedicate themselves to the gods can invoke their divine power through sacred rituals and prayers, receiving celestial guidance and protection in return.

Devoted followers of specific gods utilize prayer and ritual as conduits to call upon divine power, often engaging in elaborate ceremonies that involve the presentation of sacred objects, the recitation of prayers and chants, and the performance of time-honored rites to honor their chosen deity. By invoking the divine power of their god, priests and paladins find themselves guided and safeguarded in their endeavors, receiving blessings for themselves and their allies. Additionally, their divine patrons may grant them the ability to cast celestial spells, empowering them to heal wounds, repel adversaries, or cleanse areas tainted by corruption.

In some instances, the gods may also endow their followers with extraordinary abilities or gifts, tangible symbols of their divine favor. Such gifts may manifest as the ability to detect evil, resistance to certain forms of damage, or even the power to transform into celestial creatures.

A second source of divine magic in Amra lies in the realm of nature spirits. These ethereal beings are thought to dwell within every facet of the natural world, inhabiting trees, rivers, and animals. Priests and druids who practice divine magic through nature spirits call upon their power via intricate rituals and offerings, seeking their protection and blessing.

Belief in these nature spirits is pervasive throughout Amra, with many considering them to be manifestations of Basaj, the God of Nature. In the western reaches, however, these spirits are viewed as independent entities, separate from Basaj. Practitioners who invoke the power of nature spirits through divine magic emphasize ritual and offering to connect with these ethereal beings.

During these ceremonies, offerings such as food or incense are presented to the nature spirits, who in turn offer their guidance, protection, and blessings. Additionally, practitioners may perform specific rites to honor these spirits, express gratitude for their presence, and petition for their assistance with particular tasks.

Priests and druids who wield divine magic rooted in nature spirits often possess unique abilities and spells, enabling them to interact with and gain the favor of these enigmatic entities. These spells may allow them to communicate with animals, summon creatures of nature, or seek their help for specific purposes.

Lastly, some adherents of divine magic believe that faith and conviction serve as its wellspring. In this interpretation, divine magic is an expression of the practitioner's belief and willpower. Monks and paladins who practice divine magic through their faith and conviction can call forth divine power through prayer and meditation, using their determination to bless or curse others.

Monastic orders often channel this form of magic through the manipulation of Ki energy. Ki represents the vital force that courses through the body, a power that can be harnessed to enhance health, focus, and physical prowess. Like divine magic, the practice of Ki necessitates unwavering faith and conviction in one's ability to control and channel energy.

Meditation is a cornerstone of both disciplines, providing a means for practitioners to connect with their divinity, helping to connect the humane and the divine realms.

## Campaign Use

- Divine magic can prove devotion, reveal hypocrisy, or make religious politics tangible.
- Nature-spirit traditions allow western rites and druidic circles to differ from temple practice.
- Ki traditions give monks and paladins a path grounded in faith, discipline, and will.

title: Schools of Magic
subTitle: The ten known disciplines of arcane practice, including secret traditions beyond ordinary player reach.
body:
## Overview

The schools of magic are the main intellectual map by which Amran mages classify spells, dangers, and apprenticeships.

### The School of Abjuration

Centered on protection and defense, this school's spells safeguard its practitioners and their allies from harmful magical effects and damage.

### The School of Divination

Focused on the perception and understanding of both the magical and physical worlds, this school's spells grant insights into the hidden truths of reality and glimpses of the future.

### The School of Conjuration

Dedicated to the summoning of magical creatures and items, this school's spells call forth powerful beings and artifacts to aid their practitioners in achieving their aims.

### The School of Enchantment

Concentrated on the manipulation of minds and emotions, this school's spells exert influence over the thoughts and actions of others.

### The School of Evocation

Emphasizing the creation and control of raw magical energy, this school's spells unleash devastation upon enemies and obliterate obstacles in their path.

### The School of Illusion

Committed to the crafting of illusions and the manipulation of perception, this school's spells deceive and bewilder foes.

### The School of Necromancy

Devoted to the mastery of life and death, this school's spells summon and control the deceased while also providing healing and restoration.

### The School of Transmutation

Focused on the alteration of matter and energy, this school's spells transmute objects and creatures, changing their forms in myriad ways.

### The School of Chronomancy

Dedicated to the manipulation of time, this school allows mages to control and alter the flow of time in the past, present, and future. Chronomancy spells are used to foresee the future, alter history, accelerate or decelerate time, and manipulate timelines.

### The School of Entropy

Committed to degradation and chaos, this school enables mages to manipulate the entropy of energy in their environment. Entropic Magic spells are used to disintegrate objects, hasten decay and rot, and increase the amount of chaos and disorder in the affected area.

## Restricted Traditions

Chronomancy and Entropy are deliberately rare. In Amra lore, elves and ancient magicians kept these schools secret because their powers can distort time, decay, history, and the stability of reality itself.
