## Overview

In the mists of the ancient past, the elven realm of Tel'Dovan was a land steeped in turmoil and strife. The noble houses, each driven by their own ambitions and desires, were locked in a perpetual cycle of conflict, the once-proud elven kingdom mired in the throes of civil war. It was in these turbulent times that a young leader, Gaeladrian Korianthos, would rise to prominence and forever change the course of history.

Gaeladrian Korianthos was a beacon of wisdom and courage amidst the chaos. Even in his youth, he demonstrated an innate talent for warfare and military strategy, earning the respect and allegiance of those who once sought his downfall. With unwavering determination, he united the warring noble houses under his rule, ushering in an era of peace and prosperity for the beleaguered elves of Tel'Dovan.

Under King Korianthos' reign, Tel'Dovan emerged as a force to be reckoned with, its elven archers unparalleled in their mastery of the bow and arrow. The king himself honed the tactical acumen of his military commanders, guiding them in the art of devising cunning and innovative battle plans. The once-fragmented kingdom was transformed into a bastion of safety and wealth, where noble houses thrived and the people knew a harmony long thought lost.

Yet, as the human race began to spread across the continent, the elves of Tel'Dovan grew wary of this new potential threat. King Korianthos, ever the pragmatic leader, recognized that the elves were vastly outnumbered by the humans. Undaunted, he devised a daring and unconventional strategy: sending small, elite units deep into enemy territory to sow discord and chaos.

The king's gambit proved successful, forcing the humans to wage war on multiple fronts. However, after years of relentless conflict, the elven forces began to suffer significant losses. King Korianthos, ever perceptive, saw that continuing the fight against the humans was a futile endeavor. Thus, he made a decision that would send shockwaves throughout elven society: to cede elven lands to the humans.

In an unprecedented move, King Gaeladrian Korianthos of Tel'Dovan initiated the Hundred Nights' Capitulations, driven by a desire to ensure the safety and security of his kingdom. To the south of the elven realm, the Peaks of the Draken had become infested with menacing creatures, and the elven military was stretched thin by the protracted war against the human kingdom of Castell. King Korianthos believed that establishing peace and cooperation with the humans would not only protect his people from external threats but also aid in rebuilding the elven military.

Furthermore, King Korianthos envisioned a future where humans and elves could work together, advancing the fields of medicine and technology and fostering a more just and equitable society. Though the kingdom of Tel'Areth vehemently opposed The Capitulations of the Hundred Nights, King Korianthos remained steadfast in his conviction that it was the best course of action to protect his people and preserve peace within the region.

## Politics

### House Korianthos

Deep within the verdant heart of the ancient elven kingdom of Tel'Dovan lies House Korianthos, a bastion of military might and strategy, and one of the most enduring and influential of the realm's noble families. Bearing a lineage that stretches back to the dawning of the realm, the members of this esteemed house have long held positions of prominence in the king's court, their martial skill and cunning commanding both respect and fear.

Under the reign of king Gaeladrian Korianthos, one of the most renowned military tacticians of Amra, House Korianthos has continued to uphold its storied reputation, its soldiers standing as a bulwark against the encroaching darkness that threatens to consume the lands of Tel'Dovan. The warriors of House Korianthos are as fierce as they are disciplined, their hearts tempered by the fires of their ancestors' indomitable spirit.

But the warriors of House Korianthos are more than mere soldiers; they are stewards of the land, protectors of nature, and skilled hunters, their symbiotic relationship with the region's druids forming an integral part of their storied heritage. Together, the two forces strive to maintain the delicate balance between the mortal and natural worlds, safeguarding the realm's verdant heart and ensuring its continued prosperity.

However, despite the many accolades and accomplishments that have earned House Korianthos its place of prestige in Tel'Dovan's annals, there lies a darker history of internal strife and long-standing rivalries. The most notable of these has been the simmering tension between House Korianthos and House Valtari, an elven house revered for its expertise in healing and herbology. Though both families have forged a tenuous alliance in recent years, the ghosts of their shared past continue to haunt the halls of their respective strongholds, their memories as unyielding as the ancient trees that surround them.

House Korianthos' tumultuous history is further marred by its involvement with the human kingdom of Castell, a transgression that has earned the lasting enmity of the elven kingdom of Tel'Areth and its ruler, King Arathiel Aelarion. This unforgivable alliance with the humans has become a stain upon House Korianthos' reputation, one that the other noble houses of Tel'Dovan have been unable to forget.

In the quiet moments that punctuate the chaos of war and intrigue, the members of House Korianthos find solace in the deep roots of their ancient homeland, the whispers of the wind in the trees and the gentle murmurs of the rivers a reminder of the beauty and peace that still endure amid the darkness.

With each passing day, House Korianthos seeks to forge a new path forward, one that will allow them to atone for their past transgressions and rebuild the trust and respect that has been so sorely tested. In the hearts of the youngest members of the house, hope flickers like a beacon, a flame that burns with the promise of a brighter future.

Yet, in the shadows that still cling to the corners of the realm, the specters of the past linger, a constant reminder of the mistakes and missteps that have led House Korianthos to its present juncture. It is a battle not only for redemption, but also for the very soul of their noble house, each member striving to balance the weight of their ancestry with the pressing need to adapt and evolve.

As the warriors of House Korianthos look toward the horizon, they must now contend with the ever-shifting landscape of Tel'Dovan politics and the looming threat of ancient enemies long thought vanquished. Their resilience and strength will be tested in the fires of conflict, their allegiances and loyalties strained by the demands of duty and the temptations of power.

In this time of turmoil, the members of House Korianthos must come together, their bonds of kinship and shared heritage forging a new path forward, one that honors the sacrifices of their forebears while embracing the opportunities and challenges that lie ahead. It is a journey that will take them from the verdant heart of Tel'Dovan to the farthest reaches of Amra, each step a testament to their courage and their commitment to the realm they have sworn to protect.

With each passing season, House Korianthos continues to adapt and grow, the hard lessons of their past serving as a crucible for the forging of a stronger, more unified family. As they march beneath the banner of their proud heritage, they remain ever vigilant, their eyes focused on the promise of a better tomorrow, even as the shadows of their history threaten to consume them.

### House Valtari

In the storied annals of Tel'Dovan, few noble houses have inspired as much reverence and admiration as House Valtari, a lineage of renowned healers and herbalists whose mastery of the healing arts has brought relief to countless souls. For generations, they have stood at the forefront of Tel'Dovan's medical advancements, earning the enduring loyalty and gratitude of its people. Yet, despite their esteemed legacy, House Valtari has long been embroiled in a bitter rivalry with the illustrious House Korianthos.

The tension between these ancient houses came to a head during the reign of King Gaeladrian Korianthos. When the king convened the historic Capitulations of the Hundred Nights—a summit to cede part of the elven ancestral lands to humans in exchange for peace and a shared defense against the perils of the Draken Peaks—House Valtari emerged as the strongest opposition. Viewing the agreement as a betrayal of elven sovereignty, they argued that reliance on human alliances would only weaken the elven resolve to defend their lands.

As the debates grew more intense, a faction within House Valtari devised a desperate plan to oppose the king’s decision. Believing that King Gaeladrian's rule endangered the elven realm's autonomy, they attempted a coup d'état, seeking to unseat the monarch and overturn the Capitulations. However, their plans were discovered and swiftly crushed by loyalist forces. Though the masterminds behind the coup eluded capture, the shadow of their treachery cast a long pall over House Valtari. Branded as traitors, the once-revered house was stripped of its influence and banished to the fringes of Tel'Dovan society.

Despite their fall from grace, House Valtari refused to fade into obscurity. Determined to reclaim their honor, they focused on winning back the trust of the people through their unparalleled expertise in the healing arts. Over decades, they rebuilt their reputation, earning the loyalty of the common folk and amassing resources to stage their resurgence. In time, their pragmatic leaders negotiated an uneasy alliance with their longtime rivals, House Korianthos, recognizing the need for unity against a growing external threat.

Yet, old wounds linger, and the alliance remains fraught with suspicion and mistrust. In the council halls of Tel'Dovan, echoes of the coup and the bitter feuds that shaped the realm's history continue to influence elven politics. As a new era dawns, House Valtari and House Korianthos stand at the forefront of Tel'Dovan's destiny, their shared path marred by a history that neither can fully escape.

Here, scions of House Liriel are forged in the crucible of battle, learning from a tender age the art of hand-to-hand combat as they hone their prowess with sword, spear, and shield. Throughout the annals of history, House Liriel has marshaled Tel'Dovan's armies in innumerable conflicts, staunchly defending the realm's frontiers and expanding its dominion through hard-won military triumphs. Yet, House Liriel has not been without its detractors. The steadfast rigidity of its battle doctrines and its reluctance to adapt to novel tactics or strategies has, on occasion, led to avoidable setbacks and squandered opportunities in the theater of war. Undeterred by such criticism, House Liriel remains resolute in its adherence to its time-honored ways, trusting in its unwavering discipline and formidable martial prowess to see them through.

An intriguing facet of the political and martial landscape of Tel'Dovan is the spirited rivalry that exists between House Liriel and House Korianthos. Behind their imposing front and military prowess, House Liriel holds a quiet reverence for the Valoris range they call home.
