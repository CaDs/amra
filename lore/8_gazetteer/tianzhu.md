## Overview

In the heart of a continent teeming with history and mystique, cradled within a diverse landscape of rugged mountains, sweeping plains, and sheltered valleys, lies the storied realm of Tianzhu. This ancient land, long an epicenter of trade and culture, owes its prominence to its favorable locale and bountiful natural wealth. The capital city, Cathai, gleams like a jewel encrusted in the embracing mountainside, mirrored in the tranquil surface of the Copper Lake below, a testament to its renown as a marvel among the world's great cities.

Nestled within the realm's heart is the shimmering expanse of the Copper Lake, an opulent reservoir of valuable ores, not least among them the eponymous copper that pulses through the land's lifeblood. These prosperous veins have long since lined the coffers of the ruling dynasty, financing their endeavors to safeguard and expand the borders of Tianzhu.

A lineage divine in descent, the imperial family has governed Tianzhu for countless generations. The reigning dynasty, the esteemed Zhou, have maintained an unbroken chain of rule for over four centuries, their sagacious leadership fostering a climate of stability and prosperity that commands the deep respect of their subjects.

Yet, beneath the tranquil façade of this realm, lurks the memory of blood and steel. The saga of the Battle of Dragon Plain, a pitched conflict fought in a time long past, echoes through the halls of history. The orcish horde, led by the fearsome Warchief Skarr, had swept down from the north, their intentions set on the conquest of Tianzhu. Yet, they were met on the battlefield by the indomitable Zhou forces, led by the valiant Emperor Shi.

In the face of overwhelming odds, the Zhou fought with a tenacity borne of desperation and courage. The battle raged, the verdant plains of Dragon Plain stained crimson with the blood of the fallen. Eventually, through determination and superior strategy, the Zhou turned the tide, achieving a hard-fought victory that would mark a turning point in their struggle against the orcish invaders.

Honoring this heroic victory, and to fortify against further onslaughts, the Zhou erected the Eternal Walls, a grand bulwark stretching from the tranquil shores of the Lake of Swords in the north to the foreboding mountains known as the Tail of the Dragon in the south.

The pulsating rhythm of commerce is the beating heart of Tianzhu, with caravans laden with precious minerals, sumptuous silks, and delicate porcelain, journeying to distant corners of the world. The majestic forests that cloak the land's rugged spine yield timber, while the serpentine rivers teem with fish, further bolstering the region's economic vitality.

Intricate networks of trade routes spiderweb across the land, each a vein of prosperity that nourishes the body of the realm. The markets of Cathai bustle with vibrant activity, an orchestration of diverse sounds, sights, and scents. Traders, artisans, and scholars mingle, their collective energy lending a pulsating vibrancy to the city's air.

In the present day, Tianzhu stands as a vibrant testament to its past triumphs and enduring spirit. The Zhou continue their just rule, maintaining an environment that fosters the growth of artistry, commerce, and spirituality.

## Politics

### Zhou Family

In the annals of time, the origins of the Zhou Dynasty are shrouded in the mystic aura of ancient legend. Their genesis is intertwined with the tale of the Warrior Emperor, a figure of immense stature and indomitable will, who emerged from the tumultuous era when Tianzhu was a fractured expanse of nomadic tribes, each a silo unto itself, steeped in internecine warfare and strife.

The Warrior Emperor, whose true name is lost to antiquity, was a son of the steppe, a child of the wind and open sky. His prowess in martial arts was unmatched, his keen intellect a beacon in those dark times, and his indomitable spirit inspired awe and loyalty among the scattered tribes of Tianzhu. With sheer will, martial prowess, and an unyielding vision, he rallied the scattered tribes under a single banner, forging the bedrock of what would become the Tianzhu Empire.

However, the path to unity was fraught with challenges and adversity. The Zhou faced formidable enemies who staunchly defended their autonomy, unwilling to bend the knee. Chief among them was the Yuan Clan, a tribe known for their brutal warriors and beastmasters who commanded ferocious creatures of the steppe. They fought fiercely, a raging tempest against the tide of unity that the Warrior Emperor represented.

To the east, the Qiang tribe, masters of horse and bow, mounted significant opposition, their lightning-fast raids wreaking havoc across the territories newly unified under Zhou rule. Their guerrilla warfare tactics tested the strategic genius of the Zhou, fostering a tradition of innovative military strategy that would become a defining trait of the dynasty.

Among the many trials the Zhou faced, none was more harrowing than the conflict with the Shadow Walkers, an elusive tribe said to have mastered the arcane arts. They manipulated the very elements and conjured terrifying creatures from the ethereal planes.

The battle against this arcane tribe was a battle against an enemy that twisted the reality around them, making it one of the toughest challenges faced by the young Zhou Dynasty.

Each victory was hard-fought, paid for with the blood and sweat of Zhou's bravest. Each conquered tribe added a new facet to the emerging Empire, reinforcing it against the trials to come. It was these early struggles that laid the foundation for the Zhou Dynasty, forging it in the crucible of conflict and shaping its destiny. These ancient battles defined their character, their unyielding spirit, their boundless resilience, and their insatiable pursuit of unity, peace, and prosperity. The tales of their victories are still sung by the bards and scribes of Tianzhu, a testament to a legacy that lives on, echoed in the heartbeat of the Empire.

In today's world of shadowy politics and secret alliances, the Zhou Dynasty is under siege not by marauding warriors or arcane tribes but by a more insidious adversary - subterfuge. The Celestial Court, once a bastion of harmony and enlightenment, now teems with undercurrents of intrigue and ambition. The gilded halls echo with whispered secrets and veiled threats, as courtiers plot and scheme under a veneer of courteous smiles and deferential bows.

The machinations of the Liang family, stand as a looming threat to the Zhou's rule. Possessing significant sway over the military and the economy, their influence is pervasive. The Jade Phoenix's ambitions are as resilient and daring as their namesake, they subtly erode the Zhou's control, aiming to place a puppet ruler on the celestial throne. The Liang's strategy is a slow yet relentless pursuit, a creeping shadow over the Emperor's reign.

Meanwhile, the nefarious Cult of the Blood Tiger gnaws away at the roots of the Celestial Court. With their dark magic and abhorrent practices, they are a stark contrast to the court's cultivated refinement. The cult has infiltrated the deepest corners of the court, its tentacles stretching unseen through the palace halls. They labor in the shadows to weaken the Emperor, plotting to seize control in a gambit fueled by dark rites and insatiable ambition.

Externally, maintaining peace and diplomacy with neighboring nations presents an additional layer of complexity. Tianzhu's prosperity has drawn the covetous gaze of many kingdoms. The lands beyond the Eternal Walls are not merely a wilderness to be tamed but are home to proud and ancient civilizations with their aspirations and grievances. Ranging from cordial relations with the Kingdom of Alkebu , fraught with historic trade disputes, to an uneasy truce with the Orc tribes of the Iron Steppes, managing the fragile balance of power is a dance on a razor's edge.

### Liang Family

In the storied realm of Amra, amidst the vast and wondrous landscapes of Tianzhu, lies an intricate tale of the Liang family, whose legacy stretches across generations, as inexorable as the rivers that crisscross the land. The Liang lineage traces its roots to the resourceful merchant, Liang Jian, a man whose cunning and expertise in the art of trade caught the eye of the reigning Emperor of the Zhou dynasty.

In gratitude for his invaluable services, Liang Jian was granted a prestigious position as adviser to the emperor, securing his family's place as one of the most influential within the Zhou imperial court. Over time, the Liang family flourished, and their expertise in diplomacy and trade proved to be the key to their ever-growing influence.

As the architects of trade relations with distant lands and neighboring kingdoms, the Liang family became indispensable in negotiations and the workings of internal commerce. Their astute political skill and unyielding determination ensured the family's continued prominence within the Zhou imperial court, as they deftly navigated the treacherous waters of power and influence.

To maintain their grip on power, the Liang family wove intricate webs of alliances and political machinations, delving into the realms of dark magic and employing subterfuge to manipulate their rivals. The family's legacy in the region of Tianzhu remains an essential thread in the tapestry of Amra's rich history.

Their impressive wealth, accumulated through generations of cunning trade and shrewd investments, enabled the Liang to extend their influence beyond the realm of diplomacy and into the sphere of military might. Over time, they began to assert their influence over the Imperial army, slowly gaining control over a significant portion of its ranks. High-ranking officers, many of whom were indebted to the Liang's largesse, pledged their loyalty to the family, weaving an intricate layer of military influence into the tapestry of their power.

Yet, the Liang family's ascent was not without its perils. Numerous rivals sought to undermine their authority, creating a constant state of tension within the imperial court. However, the Liang were as resilient as they were cunning. Employing a combination of shrewd political manoeuvres, strategic alliances, and covert threats, they managed to quell any dissension and keep their adversaries at bay.

Even in the present day, the Liang family remains a formidable entity within the intricate chess game of the Celestial Court. Despite the turbulence and challenges that ripple across the courtly landscape, the Liang continue to steer the currents of power in their favor.

As a result, the Liang family's saga serves as a stark reminder of the delicate balance between ambition and virtue, illuminating the fraught path that those in power must navigate to maintain their ascendancy in the treacherous realm of politics and power.
